Studio thesis

A home should feel composed before it is decorated.

We work with private rooms that need fewer statements and stronger decisions: the right opening, the right shadow, the right material left alone.

Rooms with a slower pulse.

Each commission begins with atmosphere, then becomes architecture: openings, volumes, stone, timber, and the space between them.

Material register

Selected for the way they age in light.

Our palette is narrow by design. Stone, timber, plaster, linen, and shadow carry more depth than a room full of decorative gestures.

  • StoneHoned or brushed, with enough variation to keep the room alive.
  • TimberUsed as volume, threshold, and weight rather than surface ornament.
  • TextileLinen, wool, and boucle selected for filtration, weight, and quiet touch.

The work is mostly removal.

Every commission is edited until the home can hold daily life without visual noise.

Listen

We map the rituals already in the home: morning routes, rooms that gather people, and corners that never quite work.

Reduce

Plans are narrowed to the decisions that change how the room feels: a moved wall, a corrected opening, a quieter palette.

Place

Furniture, stone, timber, and textiles are placed for touch, age, and proportion rather than novelty.

Begin with the room that will not settle.
